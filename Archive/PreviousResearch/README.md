## Paper 1 — Long-Period LOD and Geomagnetic Ap Modulation Correlated with Jupiter Orbital Phase

`paper1_v1_3_2026-04-12.docx` · v1.3 · 12 April 2026

Spectral and correlational analysis of three independent public datasets against Jupiter's 11.86-yr orbital phase: IERS EOP C04 Length of Day (1962–2026, 64 yr); GFZ Potsdam geomagnetic Ap index (1990–2025, 36 yr); and the Morrison / Stephenson / Hohenkerk historical ΔT reconstruction (720 BC – 2026 AD, 2,746 yr). Reported statistics: bandpassed LOD r = −0.773 (σ_perm = 21.6); Ap amplitude 3.15 ± 0.48 nT (F_eff = 13.5, p ≈ 5 × 10⁻⁶); ΔT 18-yr Moon–Jupiter beat SNR = 11.4. The paper proposes a "Polar Drive" electromagnetic mechanism (Io–Jupiter flux tube → Heliospheric Current Sheet → polar cap potential → mantle skin-depth transparency → outer-core Lorentz torque). The mechanism is speculative and not established.

**Status:** The correlations are the durable contribution. The Ap and LOD baselines are Nyquist-limited at ~3.0 and ~5.4 Jupiter orbits respectively; longer Ap baselines and direct L1 IMF monitoring (ACE archive now, IMAP from 2026) are the natural extensions. Solar-cycle aliasing is partially controlled in-paper but not exhaustively ruled out.

## Paper 2 — A Homopolar Motor Model for Solar System Angular Momentum Exchange

`paper2_v1_2_2026-04-12.docx` · v1.2 · 12 April 2026

Extension of Paper 1. F-test sequential regression on the 2,746-yr LOD record separates planetary period contributions, and the paper proposes a system-level "Solar System Motor" framework with a 17-body electromechanical typology. Main empirical result: Neptune dominates the long-period LOD signature with amplitudes 0.345 ms/day at 164.8 yr and 0.377 ms/day at 82.4 yr (F > 400 each) — 3.81× the barycentric gravitational expectation. Tidal forcing is excluded (would require 4,047× amplification). Gleissberg (88 yr), de Vries (208 yr), and Hallstatt (~2,318 yr) climate cycles are noted to coincide with Neptune harmonics. Saturn's orbital period is conditionally rejected (p = 0.010) when synodic terms are included; the paper acknowledges this and reclassifies Saturn as a gravitational rather than orbital driver.

**Status:** The empirical period decomposition is the substantive part. The motor typology (homopolar generator, Piezo Driver, Faraday Brake, etc.) is interpretive analogy rather than derived physics, and is offered as such. The Neptune-amplification mechanism is explicitly reported as unidentified.

## Paper 3 — A Candidate Periodic Signal in TT(BIPM)−TAI at the Lunar Apsidal Period

`paper3_v3_0_2026-04-13.docx` · v3.0 · 13 April 2026

Empirical analysis of BIPM Circular T data (TT(BIPM)−TAI, 2003–2025, n = 831 at 10-day cadence, 0.1 ns resolution) for periodic residuals after polynomial-plus-seasonal detrending. The paper makes two independent claims:

1. **Candidate 8.848-yr signal** at the lunar apsidal period, A = 56 ± 15 ns. False-alarm probability is strongly noise-model dependent: < 10⁻⁵ under a flicker FM null; ≈ 0.26 under the data-preferred power-law null (β ≈ −2.5, fitted to the periodogram outside the target band). Reported as a *candidate* requiring confirmation as the baseline extends past 2030 (~3.4 cycles). Anti-correlated with the IERS LOD residual at the same period (r = −0.38). The Zeeman pathway proposed in earlier versions is explicitly retracted; the coupling mechanism is unknown.

2. **BIPM barycentric formula limit.** The Fairhead & Bretagnon (1990) TT−TDB formula (~1 ns accuracy at decadal periods) is now the dominant TT(BIPM) systematic by a factor >140× relative to optical clocks contributing to TAI (e.g. NIM-Sr1 at ~5 × 10⁻¹⁸, in TAI from Feb 2026). This is a metrological argument independent of Claim 1 and aligns with ongoing CCTF and IAU work toward the anticipated 2030 SI second redefinition.

**Status:** v3.0 explicitly retracts prior-version claims at 18.6 yr, 11.86 yr, and 29.5 yr (secular-covariant artefacts in a 22.7-yr window, identified by a detrending-order sensitivity test introduced in this revision). The 6-yr Alfvén candidate is below significance (FAP = 0.15). The 8.85-yr candidate remains the empirical headline; the metrology argument is the more durable contribution. BIPM/SI standards are evolving in line with optical-clock integration into TAI, which will naturally test the 8.85-yr signal under different ensemble compositions — the paper frames this as a built-in falsification path.

## Why archived

These papers were prepared as exploratory empirical work — calibration of methodology, spectral analysis of public datasets, and significance testing under realistic noise models — alongside the author's main theoretical project. They were not submitted for peer review at the time of archival. The author makes no claim that the mechanistic proposals in Papers 1 and 2 are correct; the statistical correlations and the Paper 3 metrology argument stand on their own merits.

Potential extensions noted by the author:

- Paper 1: re-test with extended Ap and LOD baselines and direct L1 IMF data (ACE archive, IMAP science operations from Feb 2026); broader treatment of solar-cycle aliasing.
- Paper 3: monitor the 8.85-yr component in successive Circular T releases as the optical-clock fraction of TAI grows; the relative behaviour of the signal under changing ensemble composition is itself the test.
