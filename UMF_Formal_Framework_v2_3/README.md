## Framework description

The Unified Metric Framework (UMF) is a structural picture of the cosmological lifecycle that proposes a bounded universe established by a primordial gravitational event, sustained by an active totally reflective wavefront boundary, and relaxing over an extended cosmic lifecycle toward a primordial state of gravitational equilibrium. The framework offers an alternative to the standard ΛCDM cosmological model at the structural level, with the dark-energy phenomenology arising from the relaxation of the bounded spacetime at the wavefront rather than from an intrinsic vacuum energy density.

The framework rests on five foundational postulates: a primordial state of gravitational equilibrium, the universe as a bounded spacetime established by a gravitational event, the wavefront as a totally reflective inner boundary for established-spacetime content, a relaxation process toward primordial equilibrium that produces dark-energy phenomenology, and a gravitational conservation budget that replaces standard energy conservation across the cosmic lifecycle. The foundational logic is documented separately in the Foundational Logic document.

The formal scaffold introduces specific commitments: the gravitational tension field T_G as an independent dynamical field with units of pressure, the Planck force and Planck pressure as physical maxima within the bounded region, the cosmological surface tension at the wavefront's inner surface, variable electromagnetic vacuum couplings (ε₀ and μ₀) as environmental properties of local T_G value with the speed of light c held constant, gravitoelectric dark matter as a no-particle no-mass field contribution sourced by the wavefront and modulated by baryonic concentrations, and a stochastic gravitational-wave background internal to the bounded region under the reflective-boundary commitment.

The framework is pre-paradigmatic. It does not currently produce quantitative predictions that distinguish it from ΛCDM at observational precision, and substantial theoretical work remains open — including the action principle for T_G (the gating issue identified by both cold reviews), the formal apparatus for the wavefront using Israel junction conditions, the variable-coupling Maxwell sector using Bekenstein/BSBM formalism, and the specific apparatus for gravitoelectric dark matter. The framework is published in its current state to invite specialist engagement, peer evaluation, and continued development.

## Methodological discipline

The framework operates under explicit standing rules: a rigour standard of 5σ for proofs, 4σ for correlations, and 2σ-3σ notable only, applied to the framework's own claims and to citations of external data; honesty in evaluation with negative results reported alongside positive findings; full evaluation in UMF context before importing any external formalism (units, regime of validity, mapping, consistency); and permanent rejection of variable speed of light as incompatible with spacetime structure by definition. Three independent cold reviews have been performed on V2.0 and incorporated; one additional comment on V2.2 has been recorded.

The framework has been developed through LLM-loop iteration (primarily with Claude, with cross-checking through ChatGPT and Gemini) with explicit awareness of the methodology's limits. Several regressions (variable c on two occasions, substance-based dark matter language) were caught and corrected by the standing rules; the methodological discipline has been integral to the framework's development rather than incidental to it.

The framework lineage (precursor frameworks UMDT and UMGT) has conducted five pre-registered empirical analyses, with four nulls and one positive consistency match at 4σ correlation-class significance under UMDT's specific commitments. The methodology demonstrates capacity for pre-registered empirical work at the standard the field expects; UMF-specific empirical engagement awaits the resolution of the framework's open theoretical questions.
